Preventing Impacts of Climate Change





By saving energy in our own homes, the earth can be a little happier. Electricity and heat are powered by oil, gas, and coal. These release greenhouse gases which harm the earth’s atmosphere. We can use less energy by simply lowering down the heat in your home, switching your light bulbs to LED light bulbs and only using energy-efficient electric appliances, and instead of using the dryer- the sun is a better alternative when it comes to drying your clothes after using the washing machine.
Instead of travelling in your own vehicles, carpooling, cycling, walking, or using public transport reduces greenhouse gas emissions. Not only does this help reduce emissions and prevent air pollution, but it also helps with getting fitter. But, for another alternative, you can switch to an electric vehicle- Like the new cybertruck!
By eating plant-based products, more vegetables, wholegrains, legumes, and just less meat in general, can significantly lower down your environmental footprint. Producing plant-based products usually results in fewer gas emissions and requires less energy, land, and water.
We should also throw away less food- what a waste! When we throw away food, it is a huge waste of energy, and its nutrients. The food that the packaging came in also used up more electricity just to produce that plastic container and was most likely transported internationally by areophane (a big vehicle of fossil fuels). And, when food rots in landfills, they produce methane which is a very powerful greenhouse gas. So only buy what is necessary for consumption and compost any leftovers.
As we are already aware, reduce, reuse, and recycle is the most common practice we should continue to follow to lead to a greener planet again. Clothes, electronics, and other common items cause carbon emissions while being produced in factories. The extraction of the raw materials, manufacturing them and transporting the goods to markets and shops are the cause of carbon emissions. To save and protect our planet, buy from second-hand shops or by following the motto- ‘Reduce, re-use and recycle.’
We can also help the planet become a little greener by switching our electrical sources to solar paneling. Not only is it more efficient, it also generates more reusable energy for your home. Most companies have options for Wind or Solar energy, so ask your electric company and see if they have any of those options. Little by little, we can make Earth greener again.

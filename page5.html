Energy


Non-Renewable Energy






What are Non-Renewable Energy Resources?

Non-Renewable Energy Resources are sources of energy that are limited. There are only so many of these resources available, they are difficult to replace quickly, compared to how much of them are used.

Examples

  • Oil
  • Coal
  • Nuclear Power
  • Natural Gas

What are the effects of non-renewable energy resources on the climate?

  • Fossil Fuels make up 80% of the demand for primary energy
  • Two-thirds of the C02 omissions come from this statistic
  • This leads to an increase in the Earth’s temperature
  • This can lead to acid rain, which damages the soil, as well as bodies of water
  • Oil Spills & Slicks lead to the death of a variety of flora and fauna, as well as contaminating drinking water
  • Over-exposure can lead to premature death

Renewable Energy





What are Renewable Energy Resources?

Renewable energy resources are sources of energy that have the ability to replenish and replace themselves.


Examples

  • Solar
  • Wind
  • Electric
  • Hydropower

What are the effects of renewable energy resources on the climate?

  • Drastically reduces C02 emissions, thus reducing the heating of the environment and decreasing the chances of acid rain
  • Many resources are plentiful in supply
  • Some resources can use up land space and also disturb the environment (e.g.: wind)
  • Some resources use hazardous materials in their production (e.g.: solar)

Reference: Union of Concerned Scientists (2013) ‘Environmental Impacts of Renewable Energy Technologies’ Available at: https://www.ucsusa.org/resources/environmental-impacts-renewable-energy-technologies [Accessed: 1 December 2024]

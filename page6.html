Sustainable Food+Water


What is Sustainable Food Development/Production?

Sustainable Food Development/Production is the creation of food and drink, using sustainable methods, that doesn’t force a compromise, but insures this system remains for the next generation.


Examples of this practice

  • Crop Rotation
  • Organix Farming
  • Agroforestry
  • Water Management

Reference: Rachel Nguyen (2023) ‘14 Sustainable Agriculture Practices That Benefit the Planet’ Available at: https://regenx.ag/blog/sustainable-agriculture-practices/ [Accessed: 1 December 2024]


What is Unsustainable Food Development/Production?

Unsustainable food development/production is the creation of food and drink using methods that are unsustainable and deplete natural resources.


Examples

  • The use of chemicals
  • Planting the same type of crop repeatedly
  • Deforestation
  • High Water Consumption

Reference: Elizabeth Williamson (2022) ‘Unsustainable Agriculture’ Available at: https://bluebirdenvironmental.ca/f/unsustainable-agriculture [Accessed: 1 December 2024]
